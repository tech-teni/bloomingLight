History

The History and Ethos of Blooming Lights

Blooming Lights is a vision of Olufunmilola. dz Olota, made possible by God with the support of her husband, Ademola Olota. The school was founded in September 2006 at Herbert Macauley (formerly Little Lights Montessori School).

It was shut down after a year and officially started all over again in Omole Phase 1, Ojodu Berger, on the 10th of September 2007. The name changed in 2008 from Little Lights Montessori to Blooming Lights Montessori School.

It caters to children aged 3 months to 16 or 17 years. Here in Blooming Lights, pupils, teachers, and parents work and learn together. The school originally started with Creche and Nursery. The elementary department was opened in September 2009. As part of the 15th anniversary of the opening of the school in Omole phase one, which was held on the 10th of September 2022, Blooming Lights College (the secondary school arm) kicked off activities in September 2022 with J.S.S.1 and J.S.S.2 for continuity and stability in the education of our pupils.

Welcome To Blooming Lights

a whole new kind of love into your life, and you can’t imagine life without them.

Here in Blooming Lights, we pay close attention to the method of instruction. We know children are unique individuals with different intelligences, we therefore put into consideration their interests and intelligences in order to plan for all our programs. We make use of 21st century teaching tools to ensure we give our children fun experiences in school.

Education is a shared commitment between dedicated teachers and staff, motivated pupils and enthusiastic parents with high expectations. Education today is not only to pursue academic excellence but also to motivate and empower pupils to be lifelong learners, detailed and critical thinkers who are productive members of an ever-changing global society. School is fun when the approach or method of instruction and interaction is child centred and focused on helping children unfold through self- discovery, explanation and experimentation. We provide an atmosphere where our children are encouraged to channel their potentials in pursuit of excellence. It is important to us to identify and nurture the talents, skills and abilities of each child and encourage them to reach great heights as we provide a platform to enable them think, express, and exhibit their skills. The method of instruction in BLM will empower them to negotiate several issues that confront them, with the teacher being the facilitator to guide them on the journey of discovery.
